# SaaS Landing Structure Skill

## Purpose

Use this skill to structure a SaaS landing page so every section has a conversion role.

The page must follow this progression:

> Capture attention → clarify the problem → prove credibility → explain the solution → reduce objections → convert.

---

# Canonical structure

## 1. Sticky navbar

Function:

- keep navigation simple;
- keep the main CTA visible.

Required:

- logo;
- anchor links;
- primary CTA.

Avoid:

- too many links;
- multiple competing CTAs;
- complex menus.

---

## 2. Hero

Function:

- communicate value immediately.

Required:

- eyebrow;
- result-oriented headline;
- supporting paragraph;
- primary CTA;
- optional secondary CTA;
- 2–3 short benefits;
- product visual;
- proof or reassurance.

Headline formulas:

- `[Desired result] without [main pain]`
- `The [category] for [target user] who need to [job]`
- `Turn [painful current state] into [desired future state]`

Supporting paragraph formula:

- `[Product] helps [target user] achieve [result] by [mechanism].`

Avoid:

- vague claims;
- abstract hero illustration as the only visual;
- CTA below the fold;
- long paragraphs.

---

## 3. Initial proof or credibility

Function:

- reduce doubt early.

Use real:

- customer logos;
- testimonials;
- user numbers;
- ratings;
- case studies;
- media mentions;
- beta credibility;
- founder/domain credibility.

Never invent proof.

If proof is missing, use honest credibility:

- private beta;
- built with pilot users;
- product walkthrough;
- founder expertise;
- transparent roadmap.

---

## 4. Problem

Function:

- make the visitor recognize their pain.

Structure:

- problem title;
- short context;
- specific pain list.

Good pattern:

- “The problem is not [surface issue]. It is [deeper issue].”

Avoid:

- generic pain;
- fake drama;
- product pitch too early.

---

## 5. Pain vs relief

Function:

- show before/after.

Format:

- two columns;
- each pain maps to a corresponding relief.

Bad:

- Pain: manual work.
- Relief: modern UI.

Good:

- Pain: manual reporting.
- Relief: automated reports from live data.

---

## 6. Short proof block

Function:

- support the problem/solution transition.

Use:

- testimonial;
- result quote;
- mini case study;
- product proof;
- domain credibility.

Keep it specific and short.

---

## 7. Solution

Function:

- present the SaaS as the logical answer.

Explain:

- what it centralizes;
- what it automates;
- what it clarifies;
- what it accelerates;
- what it makes more reliable.

Use outcome language, not technical architecture.

---

## 8. How it works

Function:

- make adoption feel simple.

Use 3 steps:

1. Connect / Import / Create
2. Configure / Customize / Plan
3. Execute / Track / Automate / Analyze

Add one objection reducer if true:

- no credit card required;
- works with existing tools;
- no technical setup;
- setup in minutes;
- cancel anytime.

---

## 9. Intermediate CTA

Function:

- let convinced visitors act early.

Use:

- short value statement;
- primary CTA;
- reassurance microcopy.

Keep CTA logic consistent.

---

## 10. Product demo / walkthrough

Function:

- make the promise concrete.

Formats:

- demo video;
- GIF;
- interactive tour;
- annotated screenshots;
- before/after workflow.

Demo must show:

1. starting state;
2. main action;
3. visible result;
4. user benefit.

---

## 11. Story / credibility

Function:

- explain why this product/team understands the problem.

Keep it short.

Possible angles:

- founder experienced the problem;
- company observed repeated user pain;
- product built from real workflows;
- team has domain expertise.

Avoid ego-driven storytelling.

---

## 12. Pricing / access

Choose based on SaaS model.

Self-serve:

- clear plans;
- price;
- included features;
- CTA;
- limits.

Demo-led B2B:

- book demo;
- request pricing;
- explain what happens next.

Early-stage:

- waitlist;
- beta;
- early access;
- limited seats.

Rules:

- no fake discounts;
- no fake urgency;
- no invented pricing.

---

## 13. FAQ

Function:

- resolve objections.

Cover:

- setup;
- compatibility;
- pricing;
- security;
- migration;
- cancellation;
- competitor alternatives;
- maturity/roadmap.

Answers must be short and direct.

---

## 14. Social proof wall

Function:

- consolidate trust.

Use only real proof.

Quality beats quantity.

---

## 15. Final CTA

Function:

- convert after all objections are reduced.

Required:

- result-oriented headline;
- short paragraph;
- primary CTA;
- reassurance microcopy.

Do not introduce new ideas.

---

## 16. Footer

Function:

- provide institutional and legal links.

Include:

- logo;
- short baseline;
- product links;
- resources;
- contact;
- social links;
- privacy policy;
- terms;
- legal notice.

---

# Minimum required sections

Every SaaS landing must include:

1. Navbar
2. Hero
3. Proof or honest credibility
4. Problem
5. Solution
6. How it works
7. Product visual/demo
8. Pricing/access/demo booking
9. FAQ
10. Final CTA
11. Footer
